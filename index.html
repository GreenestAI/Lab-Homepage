Simon Gravelle

Postdoctoral research assistant at the Queen Mary University of London
with a PhD from the University Lyon 1.

The origin of pink noise in nanopore

We showed that the low-frequency pink noise commonly measured in nanopore takes its origin in the reversible adsorption of ions at the pore surface combined with the excursions of the ions in the reservoirs.

Find more on Nano Letters, or download the PDF and SI.

The permeability of aquaporin

Our results show that a nanopore with conical entrances and suitable opening angle provides increased hydrodynamic permeability. Strikingly, the optimal opening angles compare well with the angles measured in a large variety of aquaporins.

Find more on PNAS and J. Chem. Phys.

Osmotic nanofluidic diode

Our results show the possibility to create an osmotic diode exhibiting asymmetric water flow under reversal of osmotic driving. We show that a surface charge asymmetry built on a nanochannel surface leads to nonlinear couplings between water flow and the ion dynamics, which are capable of water flow rectification.

Find more on PRL.

Biomimetism of desert rootless plant

We show that the ability of desert plant Tillandsia to absorb liquid water while preventing its evaporation results from clever juxtaposition of a thick hygroscopic wall and a semipermeable membrane.
