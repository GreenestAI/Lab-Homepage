Graphene

Graphene has many incredible physical properties. Unfortunately its production at a large scale remains challenging. Part of my work aims at improving our understanding of the behavior of graphene in fluids, which is crucial because most fabrication processes of graphene-based material rely on the use of fluids.

Water

Water is central to my research. I am interested in the transport properties of water through porous media, the thermodynamics of water, and the interaction of water with other compound.

Image: TIP4P/2005 water molecules (LAMMPS for the simulation, VMD for the visualization)

Binary mixture

Binary mixtures, such as water and ethanol, are costly to separate. I am trying to discover new separation methods using nanoporous membranes.

Image: 50/50 water ethanol molecules (LAMMPS for the simulation, VMD for the visualization)

Disordered nanoporous material

Membrane filtration based on nanoporous material are promising solutions for fluid filtration and depollution (including the capture of CO2). I am focusing on the interactions between fluids and nanoporous media, with the long-term objective of improving separation processes.

Image: amorphous silica (LAMMPS for the simulation, VMD for the visualization)

Rootless desert plant

Despite being rootless, Tillandsia plants thrive in dry places such as the Atacama desert. During my post-doctorate in Chile, I studied the physical mechanisms at the origin of the peculiar survival abilities of this plant.
