Step 1: softwares

In this tutorial, we are going to use molecular dynamics and simulate an electrolyte confined between two rigid carbon walls. This tutorial is meant as an introduction to molecular dynamics, and no previous experience of molecular dynamics is required.

The first step is to install a molecular dynamics code. There are several good options, and many of them are open source, such as Gromacs, LAMMPS or AMBER. For this tutorial, we are going to use LAMMPS, acronym for Large-scale Atomic/Molecular Massively Parallel Simulator.

Download the last version here, and follow the instruction.

With LAMMPS, we will be able to build the atomic system, simulate its dynamics, and extract data. In addition, we also need

  • a basic text editing software. I personally use gedit, but any text editor will do,
  • a visualisation software, here I will use VMD,
  • and a plotting tool, here I will use Xmgrace.
